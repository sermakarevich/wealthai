# Meeting Transcripts

## Scenario 1 — James Whitfield (`client-001`)

Adviser: Good morning James, thanks for coming in. How have things been since we last spoke?

James: Morning! Quite a lot's changed actually. We moved house back in November — we're now at 28 Oakfield Road in Clifton, still Bristol, postcode BS8 4AL.

Adviser: Oh lovely, congratulations on the move. And work — are you still at Hargreaves?

James: No, actually I left Hargreaves in October. I've joined Dyson as a Senior Engineering Manager. The salary is sixty-eight thousand a year, so a nice bump from what I was on before.

Adviser: That's great news. Is your pension still with Scottish Widows or has that changed?

James: The Scottish Widows one is still there, I haven't transferred it. But Dyson auto-enrolled me into their scheme with Aviva. I've been contributing for about three months now, so there's probably around two thousand in there so far. They match up to five percent which is decent.

Adviser: Good, we should keep an eye on both. And your savings — still the Marcus account?

James: Yeah, though I had to dip into it quite a bit with the move. It's sitting at around eight thousand now. I'm also paying into a Vanguard stocks and shares ISA — I set it up in September, putting in five hundred a month. It's worth about three thousand at the moment.

Adviser: Sensible. And the mortgage — what happened with the move?

James: We sold the old place and got a new mortgage with Halifax. The outstanding balance is two hundred and seventy-five thousand, fixed at four point one nine percent for five years, so it runs until 2030. Monthly payments are fourteen hundred and twenty.

Adviser: Got it. Any other debts?

James: Yeah, we took out a home improvement loan with Tesco Bank — fifteen thousand over five years at six point nine percent. Repayments are about two hundred and ninety a month.

Adviser: Understood. And childcare — is that still the same?

James: Actually our daughter started school in September so that cost has dropped. We're only paying for the after-school club now, about two hundred a month.

Adviser: Brilliant, that frees up some cash flow. Anything else I should know?

James: No, I think that covers everything.

Adviser: Right, I think I've got a full picture now. I'll update everything and put together some revised recommendations for you.

James: Sounds good, thanks.
